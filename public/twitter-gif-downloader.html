Here's the thing most people don't know: the "GIFs" on X aren't really GIF files. To keep things fast, X converts every GIF into a short, silent MP4 video behind the scenes. So when you download one, you'll get an MP4 — which is smaller, sharper, and works everywhere a GIF does. If you specifically need a .gif file, converting takes one extra step (below).

How to download an X GIF

  1. Open the post with the GIF and copy its link (Share → Copy link).
  2. Paste the link into the box above.
  3. Press Download, then save the MP4.

Want a real .gif file?

For messaging apps, Discord, or most social posts, the MP4 you get will autoplay and loop just like a GIF — you usually don't need to convert. If you do need the actual format:

  • Drop the MP4 into a free converter like ezgif.com and export as GIF.
  • On phone, a free "video to GIF" app does the same in a couple taps.
  • Keep in mind real GIFs are larger and lower quality than the MP4 — only convert if a service specifically requires .gif.

Why MP4 is usually better

An MP4 of the same animation is often a fraction of the size of a GIF while looking noticeably crisper, because GIF is limited to 256 colors. That's exactly why X switched to MP4 in the first place.

GIF FAQ

Are Twitter GIFs actually GIF files?

No — X stores them as short MP4 videos for speed, so a download gives you an MP4. You can convert to a real .gif afterward if needed.

How do I convert the MP4 to a GIF?

Use a free converter such as ezgif.com, or a phone app. For messaging and social use, though, the MP4 already behaves like a GIF.

Will it have sound?

X GIFs are silent by design, so the downloaded MP4 has no audio track — just like the original.
