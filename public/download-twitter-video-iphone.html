The X app on iOS has no "save video" button, and Apple doesn't let apps drop videos straight into your camera roll without a workaround. The good news: you don't need an app at all. This page works right inside Safari — paste a post link and you'll have the MP4 in a few taps.

Step by step on iPhone

  1. Copy the link. In the X app, tap the share icon (the arrow) below the video and choose Copy link. If you're in Safari already, copy the URL from the address bar.
  2. Paste it above. Come back to this page and paste the link into the box.
  3. Tap Download. Your MP4 begins saving in a few seconds — no second step.
  4. Save it where you want. Safari puts the file in Downloads (the blue download arrow in the address bar). Tap it, then use the share icon to send it to Photos or the Files app.

Saving to your Photos library

By default the video goes to Files, not your camera roll. To get it into Photos: open the download, tap the share icon, and choose Save Video. From there it behaves like any other clip — you can trim it, share it, or set it as a wallpaper.

Tips

  • Use Safari rather than an in-app browser — downloads are more reliable.
  • If nothing downloads, the post may not contain a video (some are images or text only).
  • Long videos take a moment to process. Give it a few seconds before retrying.

iPhone FAQ

Why can't I save X videos directly on iPhone?

The X app has no built-in save button for videos. A browser-based downloader like this one fills the gap — and because it runs in Safari, there's no app to install.

Where do the videos save?

They land in Safari's Downloads (the download arrow near the address bar). Open the file and tap the share icon to move it into Photos or Files.

Do I need an app from the App Store?

No. Everything happens in the browser, so you avoid ad-heavy apps and extra permissions.
